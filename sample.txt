Within the quiet scriptorium the monks bent over their vellum, grinding pigment and laying gold so that the words might shine across the centuries. Each page began with a single towering letter, a gateway through which the reader entered the text.

Today the same impulse survives in a different medium. A terminal is our parchment, box-drawing characters our marginal vines, and the humble escape code our gold leaf. The craft endures even as the tools change.

In the margins, half-forgotten, a menagerie still plays: a snail advances on a knight, a hare beats a drum, an owl regards the reader with patient scorn. They mean nothing, and so they mean everything.
